The HelpMed Group

There are many people in our society who cannot afford the high cost of medications, particularly those who are uninsured or underinsured. This often results in individuals not taking their prescribed medications, which can lead to serious health consequences. Additionally, there is a significant amount of medication waste that occurs due to the over-prescription of medication or when individuals do not finish their entire prescription.

Our Mission is to recycle medicines, thereby reducing the medical waste and at the same time reducing the medical costs incurred by the under privileged community

We often are left over with surplus medicines. Many a times, we do not consume all the medicines from a bottle or a strip and then are faced with a dilemma as to what to do with them. On the other hand there are several people who are in need of such expensive medicines in the course of their treatment, but cant afford to buy these. ShareMeds was brainchild of three young boys: Urjil, Youg and Haard. They understood the dilemma faced by those who had surplus medicines as well as those who are medicine deficit. Our Mission

  • To bridge the gap between surplus community and deficit community
  • To ensure that surplus medicine is utilised in the correct manner
  • To ensure that the underprivilege community is not devoid of proper medical care.
  • To reduce medical waste

WE NEED YOU!!

Any one can participate

Whether you are a school going kid, or a senior citizen, You can volunteer help our cause in the following ways

  • Install drop boxes at your locality and society after taking due permissions
  • Collect medicines from your drop boxes and send it to main collection center, in Mumbai
  • Organize collection drives
  • Donate the medicines to the respective destination
  • Get ideas and suggestions of places where the medicines could be donated.

Our Story

We are four friends named Pooja, Shubham, Harsh, and Piyush who are deeply concerned about the wastage of medicine in our community. We seen firsthand how much medicine was thrown away and how it could have been used to help those in need. Together, We decided to do something about it. We came up with the idea of creating a website where people could donate their unused medicine to those who could not afford it. They worked tirelessly to bring their idea to life, designing the website and reaching out to people in their community to spread the word.
